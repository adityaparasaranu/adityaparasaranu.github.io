What is Veda?

Veda are the four collections forming the earliest body of Indian scripture, which arranged the ideas and practices of vedic religion and laid down the basis of classical Hinduism, and contain chants, beliefs and principles, and guidance on ritual.

The most important values in the vedas are Satya, Dharma, Ahimsa, Brahmacharya, Shanti and Prema. Childhood is the most important part of life for a human. When a child learn and practice these values in life, they grow up to a better person. Therefore every child must practice these values in their life from their childhood.
